What is a Trie?

A "trie" or a "keyword tree" is a useful data structure used by search engines, dictionary applications, and even bioinformatics programs. A Trie is simply a tree that stores various possible series of tokens. Each branch represents a different possibility.

Tries allow programs that have to parse strings or any series of tokens to be very fast. According to GeeksForGeeks, "using Trie[s], search complexities can be brought to optimal limit (key length). A Trie completes information reTrieval in O(N) time, which is faster than the alternative of a binary search which runs in O(NlogN) time." However, the fact that a Trie must store a large tree means that it is inefficent in terms of storage, in other words it has a high space complexity.
